I left Sydney not entirely sure when I'd be back. Eleven months later, I returned a different person in ways that are hard to articulate.

## Where I went

South America, Central America, Europe, Africa, Asia, and back through Oceania. Eighteen countries in roughly that order, with a lot of improvisation in between.

## What I was looking for

Partly adventure. Partly escape. Partly genuine curiosity about how people live in places I'd only read about.

I wanted to be in situations where I had to figure things out without a safety net — where the normal shortcuts weren't available.

## What I found

Most of it I didn't expect. The loneliness that hits after month three. The disorienting joy of arriving somewhere and knowing no one. The realization that "figuring it out" is almost always possible, and that the anticipation of difficulty is usually worse than the difficulty itself.

The people were the thing. Everywhere.

## Reflections

Long-term solo travel is a particular kind of education. You get very good at being uncomfortable, very good at starting conversations with strangers, and very good at understanding that your way of doing things is one of many reasonable approaches.

I came back with a longer list of questions than when I left. That felt like progress.
