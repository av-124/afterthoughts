A year before this race I couldn't swim freestyle. I owned a bike but hadn't ridden it seriously in years. I'd run a couple of half marathons but nothing multi-discipline.

Signing up for a 70.3 was, on paper, a bad idea.

## The training

Six months of swims, long weekend rides, and runs. Learning to transition between disciplines. Learning to fuel properly. Learning that your legs feel like concrete when you step off a bike and try to run.

## Race day

- Swim: 1.9km.
- Bike: 90km.
- Run: 21km. The last 5km were the longest of my life.

Finish time: something I was proud of.

## What it taught me

You can get competent at almost anything faster than you think if you're methodical about it. The limiting factor is rarely talent — it's consistency.
