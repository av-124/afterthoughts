A year before this race I couldn't swim freestyle. I owned a bike but hadn't ridden it seriously in years. I'd run a couple of half marathons but nothing multi-discipline.

Signing up for a 70.3 was, on paper, a bad idea.

## The training

Six months of juggling swim lessons, long weekend rides, and early morning runs. Learning to transition between disciplines. Learning to fuel properly. Learning that your legs feel like concrete when you step off a bike and try to run.

The biggest challenge wasn't the fitness — it was figuring out how to get better at three completely different things simultaneously without burning out.

## Race day

- Swim: 1.9km open water. Calmer than expected once I stopped fighting the current.
- Bike: 90km. The hills were unforgiving but the scenery made up for it.
- Run: 21km. The last 5km were the longest of my life.

Finish time: something I was proud of.

## What it taught me

You can get competent at almost anything faster than you think if you're methodical about it. The limiting factor is rarely talent — it's consistency and being honest about what you don't know.

Also: transitions matter more than people say.
